U.S. Congressional World View

When a country dominates U.S. congressional language — in bills, hearings, and floor speeches — it tells you something about where American foreign-policy attention lands. Not where it should land. Where it does.

Since 1973, we’ve tracked every country mention in the congressional record. The flags below tell the story. Inspired by The Pudding’s landmark analysis of the New York Times.

Each flag represents the most-mentioned country in congressional records that month. Tap a cell for details.

Global crises announce themselves as spikes in the legislative record. But Congress doesn’t react instantly — bills take weeks to draft, hearings take months to schedule. The lag between a crisis and its congressional peak is itself part of the story.

The Twist

Two Branches, Different Worlds

Presidents govern by executive order. Congress legislates through bills, hearings, and nominations. The countries they focus on are not the same — and the overlap is smaller than you might expect.
