U.S. Congressional World View

Every month, the U.S. Congress names countries in bills, hearings, and floor speeches. The flags below show which nation led the count each month, a proxy for where American foreign-policy attention lands. Inspired by The Pudding’s landmark analysis of the New York Times, this project applies the same lens to the legislative record.

Since 1993, U.S. presidents have signed more than 1,500 executive orders naming foreign nations—sanctions, trade actions, national emergencies, and peace agreements. The flags below show which country appeared most in presidential orders each month, a direct read of White House foreign-policy priorities unmediated by the legislative process.

Each flag represents the most-mentioned country in congressional records that month. Tap a cell for details.
